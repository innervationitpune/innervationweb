But what exactly is AI?

What is Artificial Intelligence (AI)?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a technology that enables machines to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence. Think of AI as a smart assistant that can learn, reason, and make decisions based on data.

Let’s try to understand AI with some simple, everyday examples:

  • “I was discussing buying a big smart TV, a watch, or a house, and suddenly my social media platforms started showing ads related to smart TVs, watches, or houses.”
  • “I have gadgets like Siri or Alexa. I ask them questions, and they give me answers. The chances of getting a correct answer are incredibly high—whether it’s the current time, who Sachin Tendulkar is, or playing Kishore Kumar songs.”
  • “I watched a thriller movie or listened to a romantic song, and next time, the platform suggested similar content based on my habits and choices.”

Feel astonished, curious, or even a little fearful about how this happens? Remember the saying, “Walls have ears and eyes.” But don’t worry—these are just some simple examples of how AI is being used. The scope of AI is vast and far beyond what we see in daily life.

Demystifying AI: It’s Not Magic, It’s Data!

In simple terms, AI is about teaching machines to “think” and “learn” so they can help us solve problems, make decisions, or automate tasks in smart, intuitive ways. It’s not magic like Abra ka Dabra—it’s just advanced math and data working behind the scenes!

The Big Question: Who Collects This Data and How?

In today’s world, every electronic gadget probably has “ears and eyes.”

  1. They Listen and Watch:
    • Your gadgets listen to your conversations and observe your actions.
    • For example, when you talk near your phone or smart devices, they might be “hearing” keywords and storing that information.
  2. They Store Information:
    • All this data is stored in huge servers worldwide, acting as the gadgets’ “memory.”
  3. They Use This Data to Make Your Life Easier (Hopefully!):
    • By analyzing your actions and preferences, AI tries to make smarter suggestions—whether that’s ads, music recommendations, or personalized content.

What Exactly is Data, and Why is it Important?

Data is information, and it can be just about anything:

  • Personal Information: Your name, address, city, country, religion, or beliefs.
  • Behavioral Data: What you plan to buy, where you want to go on vacation, what you like to eat, your medications, sleeping patterns, or even your fashion choices.
  • Educational Data: History, geography, math problems, theories, and theorems.
  • Social Data: Geopolitical discussions, societal beliefs, decisions, or casual conversations.

How Does AI Work? The 6-Step Process

  1. Collect Information (Who):
    • Gadgets listen to your conversations and watch your online activities.
    • Whatever you search using Siri, Alexa, Google, Bing, or ChatGPT is recorded and stored.
  2. Store the Information:
    • This data is stored in massive servers around the world.
  3. Analyze and Learn:
    • Example: Ganesh often talks about buying a house near the city. His GPS location shows he’s in Pune. He searches for dating sites and digital watches and reads news and tech blogs.
    • AI Conclusion: Ganesh is likely looking to buy a house, is interested in dating, enjoys tech gadgets, and follows news sites.
  4. Process the Information:
    • AI processes this data to predict what might happen next, like suggesting products or forecasting weather.
  5. Train the AI:
    • AI learns from data patterns. For example, if you show AI enough pictures of dogs, it learns to identify them. If AI makes a mistake, corrections are made, and the AI improves over time.
  6. Serve the Information:
    • AI can automate tasks for you, like autocorrecting texts, recommending products, or suggesting songs.

Conclusion: AI is Like an Automated Human Being

To build AI, we need data. There must be a way to collect this data, store it, and train AI to learn from it.

Like humans, AI has:

  • Ears and Eyes (through gadgets),
  • Memory (via giant data storages),
  • A Brain (through programming),
  • Experience (from trillions of datasets),
  • And it can correct itself when mistakes happen (through feedback).

AI is just an automated human-like system that learns by seeing patterns in data, practicing with examples, and improving over time—just like humans do.

Just like you do!
